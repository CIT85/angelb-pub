Aztec Calendar vs Mayan Calendar

Aztec


Purposes:
The Tonalpohualli was mainly a sacred ritual calendar used for religious ceremonies and gods. It was considered a divine calendar, representing a sacred cycle connected with their deities and cosmic forces.

Structure:
Tonalpohualli consisted of 260 days, structured into 20 periods, each with a unique name and symbol.
The 20 periods were combined with numbers from 1 to 13, resulting in 260 day names.

Mayan


Purposes:
The Tonalpohualli was mainly a sacred ritual calendar used for religious ceremonies and gods. It was considered a divine calendar, representing a sacred cycle connected with their deities and cosmic forces.

Structure:
Mayan city-states operated independently with distinct rulers and political structures. The calendar played a role in the governance and scheduling of civic events.

The Mayans assigned names to the 18 months in their solar calendar called the Haab. Each month had a specific name and 20 days.
